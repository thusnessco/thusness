# Thusness — Brand Guide

A short reference for copy, tone, and design decisions. Pair with `README.md` for implementation details.

## Name

**Thusness** — set in Helvetica italic, letter-spacing 0.2em, regular weight. Always spelled out (no "TN" monogram). The word itself is the logo.

## Tagline

**~ as it is** — sits below the wordmark, small-caps at 2px letter-spacing, `--muted`. In web contexts it may be replaced with `thusness.co`.

## Glyph vocabulary

One structural glyph: the **italic tilde `~`**.

- In the wordmark's tagline
- As a prefix on section markers (`~  Itinerary`)
- As bullets in lists
- As a solo breath-mark above the "Pillar of Success"
- As the favicon / avatar

One color accent: the **red dot**. Used once per page, in the footer, as a signature.

## Typography in one line

Helvetica everywhere. Italic for the wordmark, the tagline, pull-quotes, and the "a question to sit with" line. Regular for body. Medium (500) for the question and session headings. Small-caps tracking (2.4px) for labels.

## Palette in one line

Warm cream ground, near-black ink, muted tan secondary, hairline rule, one red.

## Voice

- **Quiet.** Short sentences. Lower-case for small labels.
- **Factual.** Describe what's true, don't promise outcomes.
- **Literary.** Em-dashes, italics for emphasis, no bold, no exclamation points.
- **Never marketing.** "A quiet hour of guided noticing" — not "Transform your practice."
- **Never hedging.** "All are welcome" — not "Most people find…"

## Do

- Use hairline rules and whitespace to separate sections.
- Let the italic question be the largest thing on the page.
- Keep the red dot rare.
- Keep lists indexed with small italic two-digit numbers (`01`, `02`).
- Let the page end with a quiet footer: domain + red dot.

## Don't

- Don't add icons, illustrations, emoji, gradients, or shadows.
- Don't introduce a second accent color.
- Don't use Inter, Geist, or other "modern default" fonts.
- Don't add filler sections to balance the page visually — let asymmetry be.
- Don't link to content that doesn't exist yet.

## The red dot rule

The dot is a site signature. It should appear exactly once on any given page, in the footer, opposite the URL. A page with zero dots is fine (e.g. a modal, an embedded view). A page with two dots is wrong.

If at some future point you want a second use for the dot — e.g. "this session is live right now" — that's a legitimate extension but requires replacing the footer dot, not adding a second one. The rarity is the point.
